Products and competitors give Alva the detail it needs to write better outreach, analyse deals more accurately, and provide sharper competitive intelligence.

## Products

### Adding a product

Go to **Context → Products** and click **Add Product**. Each product has:

- **Name** — the product or service name
- **Description** — what it does and what problem it solves
- **Use cases** — specific scenarios where this product is valuable
- **Pricing model** — how it's priced (subscription, per-seat, usage-based, etc.)
- **Typical deal size** — the usual range for deals involving this product

### How Alva uses products

- **Deal tracking** — you can link products to deals to track what you're selling
- **Outreach** — BDR agents reference your products when personalising emails
- **Email analysis** — Alva recognises when prospects discuss your products and extracts relevant insights
- **Conversations** — Alva can answer questions about your products in chat

### Managing products

From the Products page, you can edit or delete products at any time. Changes are reflected immediately in all AI features.

## Competitors

### Adding a competitor

Go to **Context → Competitors** and click **Add Competitor**. Each competitor record includes:

- **Name** — the competitor's name
- **Website** — their URL
- **Description** — what they offer
- **Strengths** — what they do well (used for battle card data)
- **Weaknesses** — where they fall short
- **Differentiation** — how you position against them

### How Alva uses competitors

- **Deal tracking** — link competitors to deals to track who you're competing against
- **Win/loss analysis** — understand which competitors you win and lose against most
- **Email analysis** — Alva detects competitor mentions in email conversations
- **Conversations** — ask Alva "how do we compare to [competitor]?" and it uses your battle card data
- **BDR outreach** — agents can reference competitive positioning in outreach emails

### Competitor discovery

If you create a [Competitor Profile (CP)](/discovery/profile-types-explained) in Discovery, found companies are **automatically added to your competitors list**. This is a fast way to build out your competitive landscape.

## Linking products and competitors to deals

On any deal detail page, you can link:

- **Products** — which of your products is the prospect interested in?
- **Competitors** — who else is the prospect considering?

This data enriches Alva's deal analysis and feeds into win/loss reporting. When a deal closes, the linked products and competitors help Alva understand patterns — which products win most often, and which competitors you tend to beat.
