title: "People Search"
description: "Find people by their job title — regardless of company — and pull their verified emails straight into your CRM."
---

Most Search Agents find **companies** first, then look for contacts inside them. **People Search** works the other way round: you tell Alva the roles you sell to — "Chief Information Security Officer", "Head of Operations", "VP of Finance" — and it finds those people across companies of any sector, reveals their verified emails, and imports them into your CRM ready for outreach.

Use People Search when the **job title matters more than the company**. If you sell to a specific role wherever it sits, this is the fastest way to build a targeted contact list.

<Note>
  People Search is a mode of the same Search Agent system as company discovery. It reuses the same activate, pause, resume, and results workflow — the difference is what you search for and how it's billed.
</Note>

## People search vs. company search

| | Company search | People search |
|---|----------------|---------------|
| **You describe** | The kind of company (sector, services, market) | The job titles you want to reach |
| **Alva finds** | Companies, then contacts within them | People in that role, at any company |
| **Best for** | Targeting a market or industry | Targeting a role wherever it sits |
| **Result** | Companies scored by fit, contacts found by enrichment | People with verified emails, imported directly |

See [Creating a Discovery Profile](/discovery/creating-a-discovery-profile) for the company-led mode.

## Creating a people search

Go to **Discovery → Search Agents** and click **Create New Search Agent**, then choose **Find people**.

### Job titles (required)

Enter one or more job titles you want to reach — for example `CISO`, `Chief Information Security Officer`, `Head of Security`. Alva matches people whose current title matches what you enter.

<Tip>
  Turn on **Include similar titles** to widen the net to closely related roles (e.g. searching "Head of Security" will also surface "Security Director" and "VP Security"). Leave it off when you need an exact match.
</Tip>

### Optional filters

Narrow the search with any combination of:

| Filter | What it does |
|--------|--------------|
| **Seniority** | Restrict to seniority levels (e.g. C-suite, VP, Director, Manager) |
| **Person location** | Where the person is based |
| **Company location** | Where the person's company is headquartered |
| **Company size** | Employee-count bands (e.g. 50–200, 200–1,000) |
| **Industry / keywords** | The sectors or keywords the company should match |

The more filters you add, the tighter and more relevant your results — but the fewer people will match. Start broader and narrow later if you're getting too many low-quality matches.

### Result limit

Every people search has a **result limit** (default: **25**). The search **pauses automatically when it reaches the limit** rather than running unbounded — this is your main cost-control dial.

The recommended workflow:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start small">
    Set a low limit — say 10 — when you first activate the search.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the results">
    Check the contacts that were imported. Are they the right people? Are the emails what you expected?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Raise the limit">
    Happy with the quality? Increase the limit on the profile page. The search **resumes automatically** and runs until it reaches the new limit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  People searches have no throttle or speed control. Pace is governed entirely by the result limit — the search stops as soon as the limit is reached.
</Note>

### Ideal Company Profile (optional)

You can add an optional **Ideal Company Profile** — a short, plain-language description of the kind of company you'd ideally reach these people at. Alva uses it to **score** each person's company for fit after import, so your best-fit contacts can be prioritised for outreach.

This is scoring, not filtering: a low score never stops a contact being imported (see [Match scoring](#match-scoring) below). The brief follows the same rules as a company search description — it can only ask for things that can be determined from a company's website, so avoid criteria like headcount, funding, or technology stack. See [Creating a Discovery Profile](/discovery/creating-a-discovery-profile#step-2-describe-what-youre-looking-for) for what makes a good description.

## What gets imported

When the search runs, Alva reveals the verified email for each matching person and imports them into your CRM automatically:

- **Contacts** are created with their name, job title, verified email, and company.
- **Companies** are created (or matched to an existing record by domain, so you never get duplicates).
- Each imported company runs through the standard [enrichment](/discovery/enrichment) pipeline, including a website summary if one is missing.
- Contacts appear in **Discovery → Contacts** alongside contacts found by other means.

<Note>
  People Search reveals **verified emails only** — it never pulls phone numbers. Only people who have a discoverable email are enriched and imported, so you never pay for empty records.
</Note>

Some emails come back marked **verified** and others **unverified**. Both are imported, but [BDR agents](/bdr-agents/setting-up-your-first-agent) only auto-send to high-confidence addresses — unverified emails are kept for you to use manually rather than sent to automatically.

## Match scoring

If you set an Ideal Company Profile, each contact's company is scored **0–100** for fit shortly after import, and given a category:

| Category | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **Match** | Strong fit with your ideal company brief |
| **Review** | Partial fit — worth a look |
| **No match** | Weak fit against the brief |

The score appears as a **% match badge** and a Profile Match Analysis card on the company's detail page — the same display as company-led results.

Key things to know:

- **Nothing is ever discarded.** Unlike company search, a low score never holds a contact back — every matching person is still imported. The score only informs priority.
- **BDR agents use the score to prioritise.** Agents work through the best-fit companies first. You can also set a [minimum match score](/bdr-agents/setting-up-your-first-agent) on an agent so it skips low-fit companies automatically.
- **No brief means no score.** If you don't set an Ideal Company Profile, companies are left unscored rather than assumed to be a perfect match.

## Editing a search

You can edit a people search's criteria (titles, seniority, locations, size, industry, result limit) while it's in **draft** or **paused**. Pause an active search first.

Saving changes returns the search to draft so that re-activating runs cleanly against the new criteria. Contacts already imported stay in your CRM — they're real people you've paid to reveal. To find **more** people with the new criteria, raise the result limit above the current count and re-activate.

<Note>
  You're never charged twice for the same person. If a search re-encounters someone you've already imported, they're skipped — no repeat billing.
</Note>

## How duplicates are avoided

People Search removes duplicates at several points, so you don't pay twice or clutter your CRM:

- **Before revealing an email**, people Alva has already returned to you are skipped — no repeat charge.
- **On import**, anyone whose email already exists as a CRM contact is skipped.
- **Companies** are grouped so each organisation becomes a single CRM company, matched to existing records by domain.

## Managing People Search with Alva

You can create and manage a people search conversationally — just ask [Alva](/ai-assistant/talking-to-alva):

- *"Find me CISOs at UK fintechs and get their emails."*
- *"Set up a people search for Heads of Operations at logistics companies in Germany, limit 20."*
- *"Pause my security-officers search."*
- *"Raise the limit on my CISO search to 50."*

Alva asks clarifying questions when a request could mean either a company search or a people search, and sets up the right one for you.

## Costs

People Search is designed so you only pay for results you can use:

- **Searching is free.** Finding matching people costs nothing.
- **You only pay per verified email revealed** — and only for people who actually have an email. You're never billed for empty results.
- **No repeat charges** for people you've already imported.

Billing appears under enrichment usage. See **Settings → Usage** for a breakdown, and [Understanding Credits](/billing/understanding-credits) for how billing works overall.

<Tip>
  The result limit is your budget dial. Start at 10, confirm the contacts are on target, then raise it — the search picks up automatically where it left off.
</Tip>
