title: "Finding Funded & High-Growth Companies"
description: "Target companies by funding stage, size, growth, and listing status — then enrich and prioritise them for outreach."

Some of your best prospects aren't defined by *where* they are but by *what stage* they're at — recently funded startups, fast-growing scale-ups, or established public companies in a sector. The **Funded & high-growth companies** Search Agent finds companies by their firmographic profile rather than by reading their website area-by-area.

It's one of two ways to search for companies. The other — [Local & service businesses](/discovery/creating-a-discovery-profile) — is best for local, service, and bricks-and-mortar firms found by area. This page covers the funded/high-growth engine.

<Note>
  Choose **Funded & high-growth companies** when you're targeting companies by funding, growth, size band, or "tech/startup" characteristics — for example "Series B fintechs in London" or "high-growth SaaS with 200–500 staff". Choose **Local & service businesses** when you're targeting a trade or service in an area, like "roofers in Surrey".
</Note>

## Creating the search

Go to **Discovery → Search Agents**, click **Create New Search Agent**, choose **Find companies**, then switch the toggle to **Funded & high-growth companies**.

### Pipeline

Pick which [profile type](/discovery/profile-types-explained) matched companies should land in — Customer (ICP), Vendor (IVP), Partner (IPP), Supplier (ISP), or Competitor (CP). This decides which pipeline and module the companies appear in, exactly as it does for other Search Agents.

### Search details

Define what you're looking for with structured filters. **At least one filter is required.**

| Filter | What it does |
|--------|-------------|
| **What kind of company** | The industry or category to search within (e.g. Artificial Intelligence, Fintech, SaaS) |
| **Location** | Company headquarters location (optional) |

### Advanced filters

Open **Advanced filters** to narrow further. All are optional and stack together:

| Filter | Options |
|--------|---------|
| **Funding stage** | Seed, Series A–E+, or IPO / Public |
| **Company size** | Employee-count bands, from 1–10 up to 10,000+ |
| **Listing status** | Private, public, or delisted |
| **Minimum growth score** | A 0–100 growth signal — set a floor to focus on faster-growing companies |

<Tip>
  Start with just an industry (and maybe a location), check the match count, then add advanced filters one at a time to tighten the list. Over-filtering early can leave you with very few matches.
</Tip>

### Live match count

As you build your filters, the form shows a live estimate — **"~N companies match"** — so you can gauge how broad or narrow your search is *before* you create it. Use it to strike the right balance between reach and relevance.

### Who to enrich (People)

Like other Search Agents, you can tell enrichment **who to look for** at each matched company — primary and fallback job functions, and your ideal economic buyer. You can also opt into deeper contact sourcing. See [Enrichment](/discovery/enrichment#controlling-who-enrichment-targets) and [Enrichment Profiles](/discovery/enrichment-profiles) for the full detail.

## Ideal Company Profile (optional, for scoring)

The structured filters do the hard filtering. On top of that, you can add an optional **Ideal Company Profile** — a short, plain-language description of your ideal company. Alva uses it to **score** how well each matched company fits, purely so you can prioritise the best-fit companies for outreach.

This is scoring, not filtering — a low score **never** discards a company. The brief follows the same rules as any Search Agent description: it can only ask for things determinable from a company's website (company type, industry, market, broad size tier), not headcount numbers, funding, technology stack, or hiring status. Alva validates it and suggests a fix if it contains something unmatchable. See [what makes a good description](/discovery/creating-a-discovery-profile#step-2-describe-what-youre-looking-for).

<Tip>
  Leave the brief blank to skip scoring entirely. If you set one, matched companies get a **% match** badge and a Profile Match Analysis card on their detail page — the same display as other scored results.
</Tip>

## Result limit (your cost dial)

This engine is **bounded by a result limit** (default **25**) rather than by geography. The search runs until it has imported that many companies, then **pauses automatically** so you can review before spending more.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start small">
    Leave the limit low — say 25 — when you first activate.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review what came in">
    Check the companies and contacts that were imported. Are they the right fit?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Raise the limit">
    Happy? Increase the limit on the Search Agent page and the search **resumes automatically**, picking up where it left off.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  This engine has **no geography spread or throttle-speed** setting — those apply only to the [Local & service businesses](/discovery/creating-a-discovery-profile) engine. Here, pace is governed entirely by the result limit.
</Note>

## What happens after import

Each matched company is imported and then runs through the standard [enrichment](/discovery/enrichment) pipeline — contacts, emails, seniority ranking, and (if you set an Ideal Company Profile) a fit score. Once enriched, companies are ready for a [BDR agent](/bdr-agents/overview) to work through, best-fit first.

## Editing a search

Edit the filters, brief, or result limit while the search is in **draft** or **paused** (pause it first if it's active). Saving returns it to draft so re-activating runs cleanly against the new criteria. Companies already imported stay in your CRM — to find **more** under the new criteria, raise the result limit above the current count and re-activate. You're never charged twice for the same company.

## Costs

Each company found uses credits, and enrichment is charged per company when contacts are found (plus the optional deeper [Tier 2 Contact Search](/discovery/enrichment-profiles#tier-2-contact-search) if you switch it on). The result limit keeps this predictable — you only ever spend up to the number of companies you've asked for. See **Settings → Usage** for a live breakdown and [Understanding Credits](/billing/understanding-credits) for how billing works.

## Managing with Alva

You can set this up conversationally — just ask [Alva](/ai-assistant/talking-to-alva):

- *"Find Series B AI companies in London."*
- *"Set up a search for VC-backed cyber startups with 50–200 staff."*
- *"Raise the limit on my fintech search to 100."*

Alva picks the right engine for you: firmographic requests like these become a Funded & high-growth search, while "roofers in Surrey" becomes a [Local & service businesses](/discovery/creating-a-discovery-profile) search and "find me CISOs" becomes a [People Search](/discovery/people-search). If a request is ambiguous, Alva asks one quick question first.
